Why bother?

Following good design principles will help you build software that is robust, easy to understand, and easy to change.

Further reading:

  • Technical Debt (http://martinfowler.com/bliki/TechnicalDebt.html)
  • Increasing cost of change curve (http://swreflections.blogspot.com/2013/09/the-real-cost-of-change-in-software.html)
- It can often be easier up front to sacrifice good design in favor of getting a job done quickly. - If you are building something you expect to scale or maintain, bad design will eventually come back and cost you more than it would have to build it correctly in the first place. - In the worst case, your project will either be so difficult to maintain that you abandoned it or so disfunctional that your users abandon it.

Cohesion vs Coupling

A good design is characterized by high cohesion and loose coupling

High cohesion: keeping parts of a code base that are related to each other in a single place

Loose coupling: separating unrelated parts of the code base as much as possible
